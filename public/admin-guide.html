Administration Onboarding

Supply Subdivision Instance Setup Guide

A step-by-step technical handbook for new Supply Admins to configure, map, and launch the Disconnection Management Web Application for their division.

Task Checklist

1. Link Google Drive Backup Sync
2. Create Contract Agencies
3. Register Agency & Admin Users
4. Set Up Zone/MRU Mapping
5. Import Outstanding DC lists
6. Export Login Credentials
7. Sync Base Consumer Data (Optional)
8. Validate Setup & Ready
01

Linking Google Drive & Photo Backup Sync

Initial connection to cloud folder system

The application requires integration with Google Drive to backup disconnection photos, geolocation markers, and field inspection receipts directly inside the folder registry.

  • Navigate to the **Admin Settings** page in your sidebar menu.
  • Under **Google Drive Sync**, click the **Link Account** button.
  • You will be redirected to the secure Google OAuth login page. Log in with the official Gmail / Google account designated for your subdivision storage.
  • Accept the requested permissions: **View and manage Google Drive files** and **Manage your Google Sheets**.
  • Once authentication is finished, you will be redirected back with a success banner. The backup storage folder structure is automatically configured.
The system secures access by encrypting the Google API refresh token inside the database. It is recommended to use a dedicated subdivision Gmail account (e.g. discon.yourdivision@gmail.com) instead of a personal account.
02

Create Agencies

Admin Panel > Manage Agency

Agencies are the contract entities responsible for sending field crews to carry out disconnection/reconnection processes. You must add them to allow routing of jobs.

  • Go to the **Admin Panel** $\rightarrow$ Click the **Manage Agency** tab.
  • Click **Add New Agency**.
  • Fill in the details:
    • **Agency Code**: A unique code identifier (e.g. JOY_GURU or M_ROY). Keep it short and alphanumeric.
    • **Agency Name**: Full name of the contractor agency for billing and reports.
    • **Contact Person**: Name of contractor supervisor.
    • **Phone Number**: Contact phone for communication.
  • Click **Save**. The agency will appear in the agency listing instantly.
03

Create Users

Admin Panel > Manage Users

You must add user accounts for engineers, managers, and agency operators. There are three roles supported:

  • **Admin**: Has full configuration access, uploads DC lists, manages users/agencies, and validates completions.
  • **Agency**: Access limited to assigned consumers, DTRs, and replacement logs. They update status, log pole connections, and upload proof images.
  • **Viewer**: Read-only access to stats, maps, and report tables.

Steps:

  • Navigate to **Admin Panel** $\rightarrow$ Click **Manage Users** tab.
  • Enter the **Username** and a temporary **Password** (case-sensitive).
  • Select the appropriate **Role** (e.g., Select agency for field contractor logins).
  • For **Agency** role, select which agency they belong to (e.g. JOY_GURU).
  • Click **Add User**.
Ensure usernames do not contain special characters or spaces. Usernames are unique globally within the subdivision.
04

Zone-Agency Map

Admin Panel > Zone-Agency Map

This links subdivision areas/units (Meter Reading Units / MRUs or Zones) to specific contractor agencies. When a list is uploaded, consumers are automatically routed to the correct agency based on this map.

  • Go to **Admin Panel** $\rightarrow$ Click **Zone-Agency Map** tab.
  • You will see a form listing all active **Zones/MRUs** detected in your subdivision system and a dropdown selector next to them.
  • Select the appropriate **Agency** from the dropdown menu for each Zone/MRU.
  • Click **Save Mapping**.
  • *If a new MRU code is imported later, return to this tab to assign its mapping.*
05

Upload Disconnection (DC) List

Admin Panel > Upload DC List

This allows importing the weekly or monthly outstanding consumers directly from your billing system via a CSV file.

  • Go to **Admin Panel** $\rightarrow$ Click **Upload DC List**.
  • Ensure your CSV file matches the standard columns exported by the billing system:
    off_code, MRU, Consumer Id, Name, Address, Base Class, Class, Nature of Conn, Gov/Non-Gov, Device, O/S Duedate Range, D2 Net O/S, Discon Status, Pole
  • Click **Choose File**, select the CSV from your computer, and click **Process & Import**.
  • The system will parse the rows, match the MRUs against your **Zone-Agency Map**, automatically assign the jobs to the designated contractors, and update the status of matching users.
Duplicates are handled automatically: if a consumer is already in the list, the system will update their Net Outstanding (Net O/S) and due date without creating duplicate entries.
06

Sharing Credentials to Field Agencies

Handing off logins to agency users

Now that the agencies and users are created and matched, you must distribute the credentials to the agency team leads.

  • Provide agency users with their specific login ID and temporary password created in **Step 3**.
  • Instruct them to log in at the subdomain URL (e.g. `https://kushida.vercel.app/login`).
  • Upon logging in, field operators will automatically see their assigned lists in the **Disconnection List**, **Reconnection**, and **Deemed Visit** dashboards. They will have access to snap pictures, upload GPS logs, and update status directly from their mobile web browsers.
07

Import Consumer Master Data Optional

Sync base consumer geolocation/addresses

This allows pre-seeding the database with structural details (GPS poles, pre-registered latitude/longitude, structural addresses) directly into the database before importing active DC worklists.

  • Go to the **Consumer Master** tab from the sidebar.
  • Click **Import Master Database**.
  • Upload your base master list spreadsheet/CSV. This seeds the system with pole associations, coordinates, and contact details, ensuring field workers can navigate to pole locations using Google Maps route guidance right away.
08

Validation & System Readiness

All ready out-of-the-box

Once steps 1 through 6 are completed, the system is fully active. The following operational modules are pre-linked and require no additional code modifications or setup:

  • **Dashboard Home**: Visual charts, pending disconnections, reconnect stats, and map clusters.
  • **Reconnection**: Handles tracking payments, logging receipts, and marking re-connections.
  • **Deemed Visit**: Tracks attempts where the consumer meter wasn't accessible or locked.
  • **NSC Inspection**: Handles new service connection requests, material issuance, and validation loops.
  • **DTR Verification & Painting**: Transformer inventory audits, asset painting registers, and maintenance alerts.
  • **Replacement List**: Tracks meter replacements, burned meters, and old-to-new asset registration.
  • **Material Management**: Issues or receives cable, meters, and accessories to contractors with inventory counts.
System check completed successfully. The application is ready to track disconnections and manage subdivision workflows.
